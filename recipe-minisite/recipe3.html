Lemon Lavender Sugar Cookies

A light and refreshing lemon sugar cookie with a subtle lavender flavor. It's the perfect sweet treat that isn't too rich.

By: Alicia Russell

Published: October 20, 2025

Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 12 minutes
Cooling Time: 5 minutes
Yields about 3 dozen cookies

Ingredients

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • zest of 1 large lemon
  • 1 tablespoon dried culinary lavender
  • ½ cup salted butter (room temperature)
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar (for rolling)

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F
  2. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or silicone baking mat.
  3. In a small bowl, combine sugar, lemon zest, and lavender and rub all together between your fingers for about 1 minute.
  4. Combine sugar mixture with butter in a large bowl and beat on high for 1 minute using a stand or electric mixer.
  5. Add lemon juice, egg, and vanilla extract to large bowl and beat until well combined (about 1 minute). Then add baking soda and salt, and beat again until combined. Finally, add flour and beat until combined.
  6. Roll dough into approximately 1 tablespoon balls. Then roll balls in sugar and place on baking sheet about 2" apart.
  7. Bake in preheated oven for 10-12 minutes, until edges are just set. They will look a little underdone, but do not fear! They will set up and be chewy in the middle!
  8. Allow to cool for 5 minutes and then enjoy!

Notes:

→ It is important to ensure that you are using culinary grade lavender. Not all lavender is processed/handled the same and culinary grade is the safest for consumption.

→ A cookie scoop is recommended to help produce uniform sized cookies.

→ Store cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for 3-4 days, in the fridge for 5-7 days, or in the freezer for up to 3 months
