Minestrone Soup

A healthy soup recipe filled with fresh veggies. A perfect pairing with fresh sourdough to soak up the richly seasoned broth.

By: Alicia Russell

Published: October 22, 2025

Prep Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 30 minutes
Total Time: 50 minutes
Yields 8 servings

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 white onion finely chopped
  • 1 cup sliced carrots
  • 1 cup sliced celery
  • 1 zucchini chopped
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 5 ½ cups water
  • 2 tbsp Better than Boullion Seasoned Vegetable Base
  • 1 (28 oz) can petite diced tomatoes (undrained)
  • 1 (15 oz) can kidney beans (drained & rinsed)
  • 1 (15 oz) can tomato sauce
  • 1 tablespoon dried basil
  • 2 teaspoons dried parsley
  • 1 teaspoon oregano
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon pepper
  • 1 cup chopped spinach leaves (about 2 small handfuls)
  • 1 ½ cups small shell pasta

Directions

  1. Melt butter and olive oil in a large stock pot over medium-high heat. Add the onion, carrots, celery, zucchini, and 1/2 teaspoon salt. Allow vegatables to cook until tender (about 8-10 minutes) while stirring occasionally.
  2. Add the Better than Boullion to the vegetables and stir until coated. Then add the water, diced tomatoes, beans, tomato sauce, v, basil, parsley, oregano, salt and pepper. Stir together and allow it to come to a low boil. Once boiling, turn down the heat to low and allow to simmer for about 15 minutes.
  3. Turn heat back up to medium and add the chopped spinach and dry pasta. Allow to cook until the pasta is done (refer to directions on pasta packaging for timing - usually about 10 mins). Be sure to stir frequently to ensure pasta does not burn to the bottom of the pot.
  4. Once pasta is cooked through, serve and enjoy!

Notes:

→ Leftovers will keep in a covered contaner in the fridge for 3-4 days - adding a small amount of water when reheating is suggested.

→ Water and Better than Boullion can be substituted for 5 ½ cups of vegetable broth if you would like; however, I think the Better than Boullion really packs a powerful punch of flavor in this recipe.
