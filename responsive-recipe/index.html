Sourdough Bread

A simple sourdough bread recipe that is perfect for beginners! The crust is crisp with a soft, fluffy inside - exactly what you would expect from a loaf of sourdough.

By: Alicia Russell

Published: October 14, 2025

Ingredients

  • 50 grams active starter
  • 350 grams warm (not hot) water
  • 10 grams of table salt
  • 500 grams of bread flour

Directions

  1. Combine active starter with water in a large mixing bowl until uniform. Then add in the flour and salt. To combine you can use a spatula, but it is easier to use your hands. Dough will look shaggy when combined. Cover the bowl and allow to rest for 1 hour.
  2. Once the dough is finished resting, wet your hands and pick up the dough on one side to strech it and then fold it over onto itself. Turn the bowl 90 degrees and repeat the stretch and fold process until you have turned the bowl a complete 360 degrees. The dough should form into a tight ball. Cover the bowl, allow to rest for another 30 minutes, then repeat this process one more time.
  3. After completing the stretch and fold process twice, cover the bowl and allow the dough to rise for 7-10 hours at room temperature. You will know the dough is ready for the next step when it has risen 50-75%.
  4. Take the dough out of the bowl and onto a lightly floured surface. Shape the dough into a ball, then gently cup the dough while pulling and twisting towards yourself. This will help form a tight skin on the ball of dough. Transfer the dough to a parchment lined bowl. Cover the bowl and allow dough to rest for 1-2 hours at room temperature.
  5. About 30 minutes before you're ready to bake, preheat the oven to 450 degrees fahrenheit with the dutch oven you will use to bake the dough inside the oven. Once preheated, carefully remove the dutch oven. You can score the dough now if you wish, but it is not necessary. Then place the dough inside the dutch oven, using the parchment paper as sling to transfer from the bowl. Place the lid on the Dutch oven and place into the oven to bake. Bake with the lid on for 20 minutes, then remove lid and continue to bake the bread for another 25-30 minutes until crust is golden brown.
  6. Once done baking, remove the bread from the oven and allow to cool for at least one hour before slicing. This is the hardest step, but it is worth the wait. The bread continues to cook during this cooling period and allows for the best texture.
  7. Enjoy!
